Introduction To Getting A New Dog?

So you're thinking you'd like a dog or puppy, and you’ve been wanting one for a while now.

Maybe a birthday, anniversary or Christmas is coming, and now you’ve gone “Perfect timing, let's get a dog!”


Jess at about 8 weeks old

Sounds so easy. But hang on a minute and read this...

It could well make all the difference.

You see, you're thinking getting a dog makes perfect sense and it could be the best decision you ever make.

However, it could also turn out to be an absolute nightmare for both you and your dog.


Meg chooses unusual sleeping place

What you can expect...

Toileting on the carpets, chewed furniture, barking at night, biting and ripping your work clothes.

It all ends up in a lot of stress, expensive bills, heartache and then to top it off, the dog is returned to the rescue centre at around 1 year old having been classed as “too hard to handle”.

Here’s the good news.

With just a little bit of prior knowledge, that dog could be a happy dog for many, many years, bringing joy into your household for the rest of their life.

Same dog.... Different approach.

That’s right. It’s not the dog. It’s the approach.

So give your future puppy or dog a chance and keep reading!
