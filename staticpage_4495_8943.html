4. Training starts the second you get home

Something that the vast majority of people don’t realize is that your training starts the second you pick your puppy or dog up.

Don’t be fooled, just because they are good when you get them does not mean that they will stay that way if you don’t know what you're doing.

Doggy Dan says:
“Waiting for problems to occur before you do anything about it is not smart. It's why so many people struggle, and a lot fail. It's much harder to get things back on track after your dog has got into bad habits.”
Remember prevention is better than cure.

Doggy Dan adds:
“Trust me when I say that dogs can turn unruly very quickly if they are not trained correctly. Just look at the rescue centers a few months after Christmas. They are bursting at the seams.”
